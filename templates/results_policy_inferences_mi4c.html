Mi 4C INFERENCE RESULTS

The text of the privacy policy is scanned to evaluate if any data inferences or conclusions can be extracted about the user from the policy text and the data collected from the device, according to the policy text.

Inferences are personal information that are not consciously provided by the users themselves, but extracted by data controllers or other third parties from given data.

Profiling/Identifying User
Data, such as name, date of birth, gender, weight, or height could be used to identify a user. Profiling is the use of personal data to evaluate specific attitudes related to the individual, such as personal preferences, interests, behaviour, health status, etc.
Location/Occupancy
The location history that can be tracked from fitness tracker data can reveal the home address and points of interest of the users. Smart home devices collect data that when analysed can reveal the occupancy status of the usersâ€™ premises.
Inferences from combining data from various sources
Data collected about the users' daily life, personality, behaviour, habits and location, can be combined with other datasets to make inferences about the user.
Inferences from disclosing data to third parties
Third parties, like insurance companies, banks or employers, could use the data collected and shared by smart home devices or fitness trackers for decision-making processes related to insurance rates, loans, promotions, etc.
Inferences leading to targeted advertising
Marketing companies may exploit user data from smart home devices and fitness trackers aiming to boost the success of their advertising programs by infering the usersâ€™ interests and providing them targeted advertising.
Inferences about health status
Fitness trackers data could provide the ability to make inferences about the health status of the users, based on the amount of activity they perform. Voice input data collected by smart home devices can disclose information related to the physical or mental health of the user.
Inferences leading to sleep patterns extraction
Data from smart home devices can reveal information about how sleep-deprived a person may be. Data collected from fitness trackers can be used to evaluate the sleep patterns of the users, sleep fragmentation and sleep efficiency.
